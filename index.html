Residual Summer - 2016

In 2016, I picked up painting again after a long hiatus. I received some paint supplies as a gift and didn't know what to create at first, so I decided to paint swirls on a canvas. I had a couple people over one day, and a friend of a friend bought it off the easel. This sale sparked the start of my journey as a professional artist.

Inc. - 2017

In early 2017, I created my own custom alcohol based inks and began airbrushing with them on polypropylene paper. I created this piece, and sold it to a friend who would later buy more artwork from me.

Calamari - 2017

Calamari was the second airbrush ink painting I created, but this time I decided to add a fine line drawing on top.

Arches - 2017

I created Arches by mixing acrylic paint with water and pouring medium. It reminded me of the landscapes and deserts of the west. Arches was the first painting I sold to someone that I had never met with the help of social media.

Drift - 2018

The Drift painting was the first ink painting I sealed with resin. The glossy finish improved the quality and durability of the artwork. This was the first painting I sold to someone outside of the USA.

Galactic Coast - 2018

Galactic Coast was another acrylic pour painting I made inspired by nature. I sold this painting to someone in Miami during a music festival. He said it reminded him of the ocean and space!

Watershed - 2019

This painting was the largest and most challenging creation I had made up to that point. I built a custom wooden frame, sealed my own paper on top, taking extra care to prevent air bubbles. I airbrushed my custom inks onto the piece, then sealed it with two gallons of resin in a dust free room I built in a lab. I then used a heat gun to dissipate any air bubbles that would come to the surface. The room was temperature controlled at 72 degrees, with 54 percent humidity to allow the best environment for curing the resin. After waiting 72 hours, I entered the room again to sand down the edges and transport it to my client. This painting was commissioned by one of my friend's customers, who mentioned she needed large artwork for her new home.

Hourglass - 2019

I created hourglass as an abstract experiment using both hands simultaneously to paint. It was the fastest painting I ever created and I sold it to someone I worked with in Los Angeles.

Love ♥ & War - 2020

This painting was inspired by a style that I had been working on for quite some time. I loved painting with palette knives ever since watching Bob Ross on TV as a child. This painting was created layer by layer, loading thick oil paint onto the knives and spreading it on the canvas. I worked on this through much of the first year of the pandemic. This style of painting takes patience and is still my favorite way to paint today.

Utopian Rebirth - 2021

I painted Utopian Rebirth for a client who commissioned it from Palm Springs, CA. While I lived on the west coast, I was inspired by the mountainous landscapes and deserts, but missed the lush green fields I grew up on in the midwest. This painting mixes both and represents new beginnings. I mixed the paint with binders and modeling paste to add more texture to the sun rays and the mountains.

Gild the Beach - 2021

This set of paintings was the first series I sold as a tryptic piece. The three paintings go together to form an abstract scene. I sold these paintings to someone I met at a christmas party in Saint Louis. I used acrylic paint and metallic inks to create them. People who have viewed it have mentioned seeing mountains, a dolphin, a nun, a basket, a beach ball, sand, and more. I like that this series inspires creativity in the viewer.

Joshua - 2021

The Joshua painting was inspired by a group trip I took with friends to Joshua tree in the summer of 2021 during a full moon. The desert was glowing from the bright moon and zero light polution. I used my favorite painting style, layered oil paint with knives to create this painting. I ended up gifting this painting to my friends who planned the trip for us.

Santa Monica - 2022

Santa Monica is the first residential mural I created for clients who moved from Los Angeles to Austin Texas. They wanted to fill their stair case with a mural to remind them of their time in LA, so I painted the Santa Monica sunset with palm tree sillhouettes. Painting this mural was challenging due to the steps and the odd angle of the ladder, but it was fun to create something so large and beautiful.

Cube Groovy - 2023

After painting the Santa Monica mural, my clients asked me to come back the following year to do a mural in their bathroom. I painted all four walls and the ceiling. I decided to keep it colorful and integrate tropical plants, wavy lines, contrasting colors, and color blocked shapes and buildings. I unintentionally painted this mural during an ice storm and power outtage in Austin, TX. I had to wear a head lamp and many layers to get through it, which I think added to the character of the mural.
